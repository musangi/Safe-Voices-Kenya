    Welcome to Safe Voices Kenya

    We are a dedicated online platform focused on supporting victims and survivors of gender-based violence (GBV) in Kenya. Our team consists of committed advocates, survivors, healthcare professionals, and community leaders who are passionate about addressing and reducing GBV. We are united by our mission to provide essential resources, support, and a sense of community for those affected by GBV.

    Read More

    End Gender Based Violence in Kenya

    The United Nations defines Gender Bases Violence as "any act of gender-based violence that results in, or is likely to result in, physical, sexual, or mental harm directed against a person because of that their gender, including threats of such acts, coercion or arbitrary deprivation of liberty, whether occurring in public or in private life."

    What We Do:

    At Safe Voices Kenya, we offer a range of services to support GBV victims and survivors:

    • Helpline Information: Easily accessible contact details for emergency helplines and support services.
    • Educational Resources:Comprehensive information on the warning signs of abuse, legal rights, and steps to seek help.
    • Survivor Testimonials: stories from survivors that offer inspiration and guidance to others.
    • Community Forum: A safe space for survivors to connect, share their experiences, and provide mutual support.
    • Awareness Campaigns: Initiatives aimed at educating the community about GBV, its impacts, and prevention strategies.

    According to the 2022 Kenya National Bureau of Statistics (KNBS) Survey, 34% of Kenyan women have experienced physical violence since the age of 15, with 16% facing such violence in 2021 alone. The COVID-19 pandemic worsened the situation, as a 2020 report from UN Women and KNBS revealed that 23% of urban women and 21% of rural women experienced physical violence within their homes, often inflicted by family members or friends.
